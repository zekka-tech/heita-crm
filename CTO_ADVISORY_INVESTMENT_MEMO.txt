# Heita CRM — Senior CTO Advisory & Investment Memorandum

*Prepared: June 2026 · Author: Senior CTO Advisor · Status: Diligence refresh (post-remediation, June 2026)*

---

## Executive Verdict

**Heita CRM is the most defensible WhatsApp-native, AI-grounded, multi-tenant SMB CRM built for emerging markets that I have seen at this stage of maturity.** The product is not a wrapper, not a fork, and not a YC weekend — it is a 1,000+ file TypeScript codebase with a 1,046-line Prisma schema, 42 production migrations, 88 unit test files, 11 Playwright E2E flows, a multi-region architecture, and a SLO/error-budget/chaos-drill operating layer that a Series-A investor would be pleased to see. Its **production-readiness score is 8.3/10** (up from 7.8 post-remediation) — an honest mid-8 — with one remaining CTO-grade gap (RLS runtime-role rollout) that can be closed in 4–8 engineering weeks.

**The investment case is real, asymmetric, and timed.** The window where (a) the WhatsApp Business API is cheap, (b) Meta hasn't shipped catalog/loyalty/AI for SMBs natively, (c) no global SaaS has stitched per-tenant RAG into the SA SMB stack, and (d) POPIA + SADC + AfCFTA momentum is creating a regulatory moat — that window is **18–24 months long**. I recommend a **seed/seed-extension** at **USD 4–6M** (R70–105M) at a **R150–200M pre-money** valuation, tied to four quantified milestones over 18 months.

**Key call: Heita is a category-creation play, not a category-replacement play.** It owns an empty quadrant of the SMB software map (WhatsApp × Loyalty × AI co-worker × PWA × multi-tenant franchise × ZA pricing). The defensibility is in the combination, not in any single feature.

### 1.2 The five vectors that distinguish Heita

1. **WhatsApp-native, not WhatsApp-bolted.** The wallet, the AI co-worker, the broadcasts, the receipt scan, the rebook reminder, the OTP — every customer-facing action lives in the customer's existing WhatsApp thread, in their language, on their existing number. No rival is born-inside-WhatsApp for the SMB tier.
2. **Per-tenant RAG AI co-worker.** A KFC Heita workspace knows KFC's combo prices; a salon's workspace knows the stylist names. Generic LLMs in Wati/Respond/SleekFlow do not embody a single business's institutional memory.
3. **Multi-tenant franchise hierarchy with cross-outlet roll-up.** Social Places owns this for *brand listings*; LoyaltyPlus for *enterprise*. Nobody for R4,999/mo.
4. **PWA + on-device OCR + WhatsApp re-engagement loop.** Customers install the PWA over a 10MB data plan; scan paper receipts with Tesseract.js in-browser; receive WhatsApp re-engagement. The combination is rare globally.
5. **POPIA + SADC posture.** South African data residency, audit logs, POPIA-aligned retention, ZAR pricing, local payment rails (Yoco, Payfast, Stitch). Global SaaS competitors bill in USD with US data centres — disqualifying for SA enterprise procurement.

### 1.3 Capability gaps to acknowledge (CTO honesty)

- **POS integration is a "bring your own" integration** — there is no native integration with Yoco POS, Lightspeed, Square, or Toast, despite the homepage calling out "no POS plug-ins" as a feature. The QR-on-receipt and OCR flows are the workaround, and they work, but a SCALE-tier franchise will ask "can I import my till slips in batch?"
- **No offline-first staff dashboard.** The customer PWA is offline-aware; the staff dashboard requires connectivity. Acceptable for HQ/staff context, but a rural franchisee in loadshedding will feel it.
- **WhatsApp template approval is the biggest GTM tax** — the customer-side WA templates must be Meta-approved per language. The README is honest about this and the env vars are configurable, but the operational cost of maintaining 30+ approved templates is real.
- **The C1 caching audit has been materially advanced.** The high-traffic public surface (`b/[slug]`, `/discover`, `b/[slug]/events`) has been converted to ISR (events now ISR with revalidate=60). Authenticated dashboard pages remain dynamic (correctly, since they are per-request). A residual set of lower-traffic routes remain `force-dynamic`; the largest documentation-vs-reality gap (public surfaces) has been closed.

## 3. The Moat — Five Defensible Layers

### 3.1 WhatsApp-first deployment moat (time-based)
By the time Yoco or Clickatell ships an SMB-tier equivalent, Heita can have 10,000+ businesses and a million+ consumer wallet entries. **Estimated defensible window: 18–24 months.**

### 3.2 RAG-per-tenant switching-cost moat (deepest)
The per-tenant embeddings (pgvector 1024-dim) capture a business's *institutional memory*. Leaving Heita means abandoning a year of catalog, FAQ, return-policy, and stock data the AI has been trained on. **Switching cost grows monotonically with tenure.**

### 3.3 Franchise-hierarchy data-model moat
A multi-tenant Postgres model with parent/child `Business`, per-outlet staff audit logs, cross-outlet loyalty redemption, and HQ roll-up dashboards. None of the WA BSPs (Wati, Respond, Sleekflow) and none of the SMB loyalty platforms (Smile, Yotpo, LoyaltyLion) do this at <R5,000/mo. **Once a franchise brand standardises on Heita, displacement = re-onboarding every outlet.**

### 3.4 PWA + on-device OCR + WhatsApp re-engagement loop
A combination nobody closes today. Yoco closes payment→receipt. Smile closes purchase→points. **Heita closes paper-receipt→points→PWA→WhatsApp re-engagement.** This is the *experience* moat — the one customers feel.

### 3.5 POPIA + SADC + ZAR pricing regulatory/cultural moat
Every global SaaS competitor (Wati, Respond, Smile, Yotpo, HubSpot, Zoho) bills in USD/INR/HKD with overseas data residency. **Every SA enterprise procurement asks "where is the data?"** Each win compounds the moat.

### 4.2 Regional read

**South Africa + Africa** — Heita's home market. Yoco is the elephant in the room (200k+ merchants, R34B+ processed) but payments-led; loyalty is a roadmap item. **Window: 18–24 months before Yoco or a bank (Nedbank acquired iKhokha) bundles loyalty + WhatsApp.** The white space is WhatsApp-native loyalty + per-tenant AI for the SMB long tail. Yoco is *partner-able* (their founder transition in Sep 2025 creates a strategic opening) until they decide to build it natively. Clickatell is enterprise-only; Turn.io is health/social-impact. **Heita's wedge is wide open.**

**North America + Europe** — HubSpot Breeze, Salesforce Einstein/Agentforce, Zoho Zia, and Zoho One dominate the SMB B2B-CRM lane. Smile.io and Yotpo own SMB loyalty but Shopify-only and e-commerce-only. Brevo (FR) is the most direct EU overlap (marketing + WhatsApp + loyalty in one stack) but lacks PWA, RAG, OCR, franchise. Wati and Respond.io are WA-CRM-shaped but no loyalty. **No global SaaS combines PWA + WhatsApp + RAG + tiered loyalty + OCR + franchise at the SMB price point.** The Heita opportunity is *white-label or partner-distribute* the OCR+loyalty bundle into a WA-BSP customer base, rather than cold-start into EU mid-market retail. Key blockers: Meta BSP status, EU data residency (Schrems II), GDPR-grade CMP, EU WhatsApp template library (FR/DE/ES/IT/NL/PL — 5-10 per vertical), Stripe + SEPA + iDEAL + Bancontact + Klarna payments rails, multi-currency billing (Stripe Billing or Lago), EU VAT calculation, and a local field-rep / partner motion.

**Asia (India, SEA, East Asia)** — India is the toughest market. Gupshup ($340M+, 10B msgs/mo), Wati ($23M), AiSensy (₹999/mo — 5× cheaper than Heita Growth), LimeChat, Capillary (enterprise loyalty), MoEngage, Freshchat, Zoho One. **Heita's R1,499/$82 Growth tier is 3–5× more expensive than AiSensy/Wati on bare messaging — defensible only if AI + loyalty + PWA are perceived as one bundle.** SEA: SleekFlow ($15M Series A) is the architectural look-alike; KiotViet ($45M, 170K+ merchants) owns Vietnamese SMB POS. East Asia (CN/JP/KR) is **non-addressable** — closed super-apps (WeChat, LINE, KakaoTalk) prevent WhatsApp-first PWA entry. Weimob/Youzan are the Chinese analog; KARTE in Japan; Channel Talk (KR) is the spiritual look-alike.

**South America (LATAM)** — Brazil is WhatsApp-commerce central (96% penetration). Stone+Linx, TOTVS+RD Station, VTEX, Nuvemshop ($3.1B valuation, 130K+ SMB stores), Mercado Pago (super-app distribution), PagSeguro/Cielo, Take Blip ($130M Warburg Pincus, ~$700M val, the LATAM Gupshup), Zenvia, Bold (CO), Clip (MX), Siigo (CO, $100M Series, $1B val). **The white space is non-Mercado-Pago-merchant brick-and-mortar SMBs in townships/peri-urban areas that need WhatsApp + loyalty + a free tier — exactly the South African and emerging LATAM SMB profile Heita already serves.** Mexico, Colombia, Chile: same pattern. Nuvemshop is the most direct LATAM threat if it pivots from e-commerce to physical-retail CRM; Take Blip is the most direct BSP-layer threat.

### 4.3 The "Meta first-party encroachment" overhang

**The single most important risk to the entire thesis.** WhatsApp Business Platform is shipping catalog, payments (IN/BR/SG), click-to-WA ads, and Flows at scale. Meta could ship SMB loyalty natively in 12–18 months. **Mitigation: own the workspace + memory + loyalty math + franchise topology layer that Meta has shown no interest in.** Meta consistently underbuilds workflow and AI; the data is on Heita's side.

### 6.2 The 5 critical gaps to fix in 8–12 weeks

1. **Enable Postgres Row-Level Security** on every business-owned table. Add a `current_setting('app.current_business_id')` policy and a connection-boundary `SET LOCAL app.current_business_id` from the Prisma middleware. **This is the difference between "we got owned" and "the static gate caught it" on the next tenant leak.** Effort: 2 senior engineers × 3 weeks.
2. **Treat remaining `force-dynamic` pages as a lower-priority authenticated-surface audit.** The high-traffic public surface (`/discover`, `b/[slug]`, `b/[slug]/events`) is already on ISR; further rendering work is now incremental rather than a critical investor-grade gap.
3. **Add a real SLO dashboard + alert runbook folder.** The SLO doc is policy, not operational. Commit Grafana JSON exports to `dashboards/`, write `docs/runbooks/<alert>.md` for every alert in `prometheus-rules.yml`. Effort: 1 platform engineer × 2 weeks.
4. **Wire PostHog + RUM end-to-end** (ENHANCEMENT_PLAN A4). PII scrubbing, consent gating, event taxonomy, funnel from join→earn→redeem, web-vitals. Effort: 1 growth engineer × 2 weeks.
5. **Enable `MALWARE_SCAN_REQUIRED=1` in prod** with a real ClamAV sidecar (the code path is ready, the env-validated config exists; the deployment wiring is what is missing). Effort: 1 DevOps × 1 week.

## 9. Recommendations to the Investment Committee

### 9.1 What to fund

1. **USD 4–6M seed at R150–200M pre.** Stage the raise against the 4 milestones above. Wire Series A pre-emption rights only at M12, not at signing.
2. **Insist on finishing the remaining runtime-role verification work in the first 6 months.** The former force-dynamic, SLO/runbook, PostHog, and ClamAV gaps are now substantially addressed; the material open risk is proving the full app green under `heita_app`.
3. **Tie 20% of the round to a Yoco/Stitch partnership MOU** (even a non-binding letter of intent) by M9. This is the single highest-leverage GTM deal possible.
4. **Demand a 12-month, named, paid-CAC dashboard by M3.** Not "we ran some ads" — a per-channel CAC, LTV/CAC, and payback period visible in the investor update. This is the #1 missing thing in the codebase.
5. **Insist on multi-region production validated by M9.** The architecture is designed for it; the deploy is not yet. A successful AWS Cape Town primary + EU-West-1 replica failover exercise is the cleanest possible Series-A diligence ammunition.
6. **Push for a single-tenant-tenancy legal opinion by M6** — POPIA counsel sign-off (Michalsons/Webber Wentzel), DPA template, ROPA, DPIA. POPIA is the moat; legal proof of the moat is the asset.
7. **Reserve 15% of the round for a post-seed AI/ML hire** (an applied ML lead for the RAG and prompt-caching work). The RAG eval harness exists, but the system needs an owner at the IC level.

### 9.2 What to refuse

- Don't fund a "build a Salesforce clone" pivot. The thesis is WhatsApp × Loyalty × AI × PWA × Franchise × ZA, not horizontal CRM.
- Don't accept a marketing-only "AI co-worker" dilution of the brand. The AI must remain grounded in a single business's data, not become a generic chatbot.
- Don't allow a US-first GTM experiment before ZA→Africa is at 10,000 paying tenants. The data-residency, BSP, and language infrastructure are not ready.
- Don't let the R0 tier be quietly degraded to a 14-day trial. The R0 wedge is the single sharpest distribution weapon in the product. Keep it real.
- Don't accept a single-tenant or single-vertical platform pivot. The franchise hierarchy is a moat; flattening it is a value-destroying shortcut.
